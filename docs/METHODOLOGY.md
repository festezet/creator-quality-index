# Creator Quality Index — Methodology

## The Problem

YouTube has 800 million videos. The platform's algorithm optimizes for one thing: watch time. Not quality. Not depth. Not intellectual honesty.

Existing tools measure the wrong things:
- **HypeAuditor** measures marketing performance (engagement rates, brand safety)
- **P-Score / Social Blade** measure popularity (subscribers, views, growth)
- **TierMaker / Favoree** aggregate unstructured opinions without methodology

None of them answer: **"Is this creator worth your time if you want to actually learn something?"**

The Creator Quality Index (CQI) is an attempt to answer that question with transparent, weighted criteria.

---

## The 5 Criteria

Each channel is scored on 5 dimensions, rated 1–10.

### 1. Research Depth (25%)

How well-sourced and thoroughly researched is the content?

| Score | Description | Example |
|-------|------------|---------|
| 9–10 | Primary sources, academic papers, expert interviews, original data | 3Blue1Brown creating custom mathematical proofs and animations |
| 7–8 | Multiple credible sources, fact-checked, demonstrates deep subject knowledge | Wendover Productions citing industry reports and studies |
| 5–6 | Decent research but relies on secondary sources or surface-level analysis | General knowledge channels that compile well-known facts |
| 3–4 | Minimal sourcing, anecdotal evidence, occasional errors | Pop-science channels that oversimplify or misrepresent |
| 1–2 | No sources, speculation presented as fact, misleading | Clickbait channels inventing claims |

### 2. Production Quality (20%)

Technical execution: editing, visuals, audio, pacing, structure.

| Score | Description | Example |
|-------|------------|---------|
| 9–10 | Professional-grade or innovative production that enhances understanding | Kurzgesagt's custom animations, Every Frame a Painting's editing |
| 7–8 | Clean, well-edited, good audio, thoughtful visual aids | Real Engineering's 3D models, Patrick Boyle's polished format |
| 5–6 | Competent production, no major issues | Standard talking-head with decent editing |
| 3–4 | Noticeable quality issues, poor audio, rough editing | Amateur production that distracts from content |
| 1–2 | Unwatchable quality | Raw unedited footage with bad audio |

### 3. Signal-to-Noise Ratio (25%)

How much of the video is actual content vs. filler, sponsorships, engagement bait?

| Score | Description | Example |
|-------|------------|---------|
| 9–10 | Pure content, no filler, every second adds value | Primitive Technology (no talking), 3Blue1Brown (pure math) |
| 7–8 | Minimal filler, sponsor reads are brief, content-focused | Practical Engineering — sponsor at start, then pure content |
| 5–6 | Some padding but acceptable, occasional tangents | Videos with 2–3 min of intro/outro padding in a 15 min video |
| 3–4 | Significant filler, clickbait titles, artificial drama | "You WON'T BELIEVE" titles, 5 min of content in 20 min video |
| 1–2 | More filler than content | Reaction videos, stretched non-content |

### 4. Originality (15%)

Does the creator bring a unique perspective, format, or angle?

| Score | Description | Example |
|-------|------------|---------|
| 9–10 | Invented a format, unique analytical framework, genuinely novel approach | CGP Grey's systems thinking, Exurb1a's existential comedy |
| 7–8 | Distinct voice, recognizable style, original takes on topics | Technology Connections' "why things work this way" format |
| 5–6 | Competent execution of established format | Well-made explainer in a crowded space |
| 3–4 | Derivative, follows trends, little differentiation | "Me too" channels copying popular formats |
| 1–2 | Pure repackaging of others' work | Content farms, compilation channels |

### 5. Lasting Impact (15%)

Will this content still be valuable in 5 years? Does it change how you think?

| Score | Description | Example |
|-------|------------|---------|
| 9–10 | Timeless content, fundamentally shifts understanding | Fall of Civilizations (history doesn't expire), Ben Felix (investment principles) |
| 7–8 | Mostly evergreen, referenced years later | Engineering explainers, mathematical concepts |
| 5–6 | Good content with some time-sensitivity | Tech tutorials that will need updates |
| 3–4 | Largely time-bound, news-dependent | Current events analysis, trend commentary |
| 1–2 | Disposable content, irrelevant within months | Drama commentary, trend reactions |

---

## Composite Score

The composite score is a weighted average:

```
CQI = (Research × 0.25) + (Production × 0.20) + (Signal/Noise × 0.25)
    + (Originality × 0.15) + (Impact × 0.15)
```

**Why these weights?**
- Research and Signal/Noise are weighted highest (25% each) because they most directly measure intellectual quality
- Production is weighted at 20% because good production enhances learning but isn't the goal itself
- Originality and Impact at 15% each because they matter, but a well-researched, well-produced channel that covers established topics well still deserves recognition

## Tier System

| Tier | Score Range | Meaning |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **S** | ≥ 8.5 | Exceptional — best-in-class, must-subscribe |
| **A** | 7.0 – 8.4 | Excellent — consistently high quality |
| **B** | 5.5 – 6.9 | Good — solid content with room to grow |
| **C** | 4.0 – 5.4 | Average — decent but unremarkable |
| **D** | < 4.0 | Below average — significant quality issues |

---

## What This Benchmark Does NOT Measure

- **Entertainment value** — A comedy channel can be brilliant without scoring high here
- **Popularity** — Subscriber count has zero weight in the score
- **Upload frequency** — Posting weekly doesn't make you better than posting monthly
- **Personality** — Charisma and likability are not criteria
- **Agreement** — A channel can score high even if we disagree with its conclusions, as long as the reasoning is sound

---

## Transparency & Limitations

### Acknowledged Biases

1. **Language bias**: English and French channels are overrepresented. We aim to expand.
2. **Format bias**: Long-form, educational content scores higher by design. This is a feature, not a bug — the index measures intellectual quality specifically.
3. **Recency bias**: Older channels with established libraries may score higher on "lasting impact" simply because they've had time to prove durability.
4. **Subjectivity**: Despite structured criteria, scoring involves human judgment. Two reasonable people might disagree by 1-2 points on any criterion.

### Rescoring Policy

- Channels are rescored every 6 months or when significant format changes occur
- Score changes are documented with reasoning
- Community input is welcome — suggest channels or challenge scores via the contact form

### Contributing

This is an open methodology. If you think a criterion is missing, a weight is wrong, or a channel is misjudged, the discussion is welcome. The goal is accuracy, not ego.
